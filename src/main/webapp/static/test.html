Sonam Kapoor Has No Interest in Being the ‘Exotic Indian Girl’ in Hollywood

2016-07-18

With two leading ladies of Bollywood C   Priyanka Chopra   and   Deepika Padukone   C taking Hollywood by storm, almost every female actor is now being asked about her prospects in Hollywood. But Sonam Kapoor wants nothing to do with the West right now. In an interview with   DNA, she said, “I would like to take my projects to the West. I do not want to be the hot Indian girl there. I have worked very hard not to be this exotic Indian girl in India. I have worked very hard to the point where I could get to do a film like   Neerja and be taken seriously.”

Image   source

While that can be considered a subtle dig at her contemporaries, it seems like she’s given this some thought and isn’t completely averse to the idea of a Hollywood debut. She said, “I do not want to start all over again. If I can get something with a lot of gravitas, I would definitely want to do it. I would approach them myself. If it’s a great role, why not? But if it has something right and attracts me. For me, conquering the world is not important. For me, conquering and honing my craft is more important. It doesn’t matter what language I get to work in, as long as I get to explore something about myself and explore a character that I want to do. I will make a decision based on the fact that there’s something that I can do in it.”

She added, “There has to be something much more… There are so many roles being written, so why not? I want a strong, amazing character. Films like Neerja   have a lot of potential of crossing over. Look at how well   The Lunchbox   and   Masaan   were received. They crossed international barriers.”
